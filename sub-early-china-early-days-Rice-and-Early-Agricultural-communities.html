Rice and Early Agricultural communities

The farming of rice was one of the key innovations that enabled civilisations to grow across Asia. Archaeologists have found that the Chinese started growing rice about 9000 years ago! Rice was grown in the Yangzi valley by Stone Age Chinese communities. These people also domesticated dogs, pigs, chickens and water buffaloes.

Early Chinese communities produced drinks such as wine and beer. Archaeologists have discovered evidence of this at the site of Jiahu, a Stone Age settlement in central China.

Archaeologists at Jiahu also found flutes made from the bones of cranes. These instruments were played on a five note scale and are still playable today! Some scholars think that evidence of signs on tortoise shells at Jiahu also suggests some sort of early writing system.

Jewellery such as jade was also important in early China. The use of jade is documented from the Liangzhu culture of 3rd millennium BCE. Two jade types were prominent: the bi, a flat disc with a circular hole in the middle, and the cong, a rectangular tube with a lengthwise circular perforation. It is likely that these ornaments represent the heavens and the earth.
