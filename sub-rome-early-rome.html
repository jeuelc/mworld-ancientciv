Early Rome

Italy is a peninsula that extends into the Mediterranean just west of Greece. It has few mineral resources and even fewer usable harbors, but it does have a lot of fertile land. The Romans were essentially an agrarian people.

Italy is easily accessible to Europe from the north. There were no buffer civilisations to help protect the Romans from invaders, so from early on they felt constantly threatened. Already between 800 and 700 BCE, the Greeks and Etruscans began to move into Italy. But who did they meet there?

The name given to the early Iron Age predecessors of the Etruscan civilisation is the Villanova Culture; they lived in northern Italy between about 1100-700 BCE. They were nomadic herdsmen and technologically superior, having full use of bronze, horses, and wheeled carts. They were warlike and included a variety of ethnic groups such as the Sabines, the Umbrians and the Latins.
