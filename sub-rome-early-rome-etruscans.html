The Etruscans

No one knows for sure where the Etruscans came from it's still a mystery! But archaeologists suspect they came from the eastern Mediterranean, possibly Asia Minor. The Etruscans brought to Italy a more urban way of living. They lived in independent, fortified city-states that formed small 'confederacies' you could think of them as a group of friendly states.

The Latins who founded Rome were an Italic people who lived in a small village south of the Tiber River and cultivated the land. They were tribal people and you can see that 'tribal' way of organising society throughout Roman history. However, as they steadily developed their city, its government and its culture, they imitated the neighbouring civilization to the north, the Etruscans. The Etruscans reacted by taking them over!

Livy, a Roman historian writing much later (first century BCE: Before Common Era), recorded the myth that Rome was founded by Romulus and Remus, as well as the tragic story of the expulsion of the Etruscan kings. Both tales tell of the desire for power that shaped Roman history, but, also, the toll of blood that Rome had to pay for it.
