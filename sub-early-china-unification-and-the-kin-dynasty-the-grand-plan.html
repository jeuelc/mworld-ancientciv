The Grand Plan

As part of his grand plan, Emperor Qin Shi Huangdi imposed rules to regulate economic transactions and make tax collection easier.

All weights and measures were converted into standard units. And the currency was standardised: a new round coin with a square hole in the middle was adopted and the old spade-shaped and knife-shaped currency was abolished.

Qin Shi Huangdi tackled roads too. All cart axles had to be the same size to reduce problems with wheel ruts on the roads.

Then he turned to writing. A common script was ordered for all imperial territories so that they could eliminate the many local script variations. The Chinese writing system has evolved a lot since then but is still very much in use today. The roots of Chinese writing can be traced back to even earlier times to early Neolithic inscriptions on bones and tortoise shells.
