You've probably seen images of the amazing Terracotta Army!

The Han historian Sima Qian says that more than 700,000 men were forced to construct the large burial mound of Qin Shi Huangdi at Lishan near Xi'an. Beneath this mound, which represents the sacred mountain at the centre of the world, there is an afterlife palace surrounded by rivers of mercury, with vaults decorated with stars and planets. The tomb is protected by trap doors and automatic crossbows ready to fire at any intruders (like a real Indiana Jones movie!).

About a kilometre east of the imperial tomb which hasn't been excavated yet four pits containing more than 7000 life-size terracotta statues of warriors and horses were found. These statues are mingqi, or substitutes, in the form of a deployed army to protect the emperor's afterlife.

The statues have individual facial expressions, real weapons such as crossbows, halberds, and swords, and they were realistically painted.

Millions of tourists visit this World Heritage site every year.
