Myth of Romulus and Remus

According to legend, reported by the historian Livy in his book Ab Urbe Condita (meaning From the Foundation of the City), the story of Rome starts in the nearby city of Alba Longa. The kingdom of Alba Longa was ruled by Amulius. Amulius fought his brother, Numitor, and sent him into exile. Amulius also forced Numitor's daughter, Rhea Silvia, to become a Vestal Virgin.

Vestal Virgins were not allowed to marry and have children. Rhea Silvia, however, became pregnant with twins from the god Mars (the god of war), and Numitor ordered that the babies be thrown into the river Tiber. Thankfully, a she-wolf saved the babies and raised them. Although later the twins were reunited with their grandfather Numitor in Alba Longa, they decided to go back to the area of Rome and built cities there. Soon, however, ambition got the better of the two brothers and they fought viciously until Romulus killed Remus. Rome was named after its sole ruler. Its foundation is dated to 753 BCE.

Vestal Virgin

A Vestal Virgin was a priestess who was like a present day Nun.
