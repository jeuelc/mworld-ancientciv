First Emperor

Qin Shi Huangdi and his minister Li Si established a centralised, violent and autocratic state which ran according to Legalist' ideas. This meant they thought human nature was basically evil and needed to be controlled by severe discipline based on very harsh laws.

The empire was organised into prefectures and smaller districts. They were administered by governors appointed by the emperor. The emperor's rule was violent he ordered the execution of Confucian scholars and the burning of books that didn't suit his ideas!

By this time the plough had been invented and agriculture was booming because of it, but heavy taxes and forced labour resulted in rebellions by peasants and these uprisings undermined the imperial throne.

Qin Shi Huangdi was obsessed with the idea of dying. Early in his rule, he decreed that a giant tomb be built for him. He also sent expeditions in search of the 'elixir of life', a potion he thought would give him eternal life, and the mythical 'Island of the Immortals'.
