The Shang state was one of several big powers of Chinas late Bronze Age. Different kingdoms had their own cultural diversity and artistic styles. You can see this from the bronze, ivory and jade items from Sanxingdui near Chengdu, or from the rich tombs of the Chu kingdom in the south.

Around 1000 BCE, the Shang state was conquered by the kingdom of Zhou.

The new Zhou dynastic period is divided into three time frames: 'Western Zhou' (1050-770 BCE), 'Spring and Autumn' (770-475 BCE) and 'Warring States' (475-221 BCE). The Zhou capitals were clusters of settlements around a royal palace. The palaces were built on a rammed-earth platform with wooden frames and ceramic roof tiles.

Zhou ritual bronzes were developed from Shang bronze types and were used in similar ways. For example, long inscriptions recorded special events.
