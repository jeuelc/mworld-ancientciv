The Great Wall of China is one of the wonders of the world. It is the largest man-made structure on Earth today. It is almost 9000 kilometres long and mostly made of rammed-earth, bricks and stone.

Originally many separate segments of the Great Wall were built by various states during the 'Spring and Autumn' and 'Warring States' periods of China's history. More than an effective defence, these walls created a symbolic barrier between the Chinese and the outside 'barbarians'.

Qin Shi Huangdi wanted a huge defensive wall along the northern borders to protect the northern regions from the raids of the Xiongnu (Huns) and other nomadic tribes. An immense labour force was then mobilised, often under harsh conditions, to connect the numerous segments of the older walls.
