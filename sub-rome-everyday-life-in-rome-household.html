Household

The head of the Roman family was the 'pater familias'.

He was the boss! He had absolute power over his children and descendants. But however rigid this authority was theoretically, it was greatly modified in practice. This happened under the Republic by custom and under the Empire by law. The head of the house usually assigned to his children property, 'peclium' (which directly translated means 'property in cattle'), for them to manage for their own benefit. And although the 'pater familis' theoretically owned all their acquisitions, in practice he was their trustee, helping them to manage them.

In marrying, the wife was separated entirely from her father's family and passed into her husband's power or 'hand' ('conventi in manum'). Have you ever heard the expression asking for someones hand in marriage? This is where it comes from!

Acquisitions

Acquisitions refer to someone's property and assets and a trustee is someone who has the legal responsibility to manage them.
