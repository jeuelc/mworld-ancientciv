Caesar the Provoker

Caesar was elected consul in 59 BCE and 'the First Triumvirate' of Caesar with his allies Crassus and Pompey came to power. Caesar embarked on a spectacular war of conquest. He added lots of territory to the Roman Empire in northern France, Belgium, and even southern Great Britain. But trouble loomed!

Pompey managed to turn the Senate against Caesar after a few years. The Senate declared him an enemy of the state and demanded that he handed over his generalship and the province he had been given. But Caesar had other ideas. His troops were fiercely loyal to him and he ordered them to cross the Rubicon River, which separated his province from Italy. This was a grave crime against the state! The Civil War started the minute his legions had finished crossing the Rubicon. Upon crossing the river, Caesar allegedly said: "alea jacta est" which means 'the die has been cast'.

Caesar kept provoking the traditional senators by demanding to be worshipped as a god, like eastern rulers were, and starting a passionate affair with Cleopatra, the Macedonian Queen of Egypt.
