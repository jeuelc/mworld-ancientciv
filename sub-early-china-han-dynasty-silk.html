Silk is a beautiful material, soft and shiny and precious, so you might be shocked to discover that it actually comes from silkworms! Although the Han Chinese didn't invent silk, they did use it for many things. People even had to pay taxes with yarns of silk!

Silk was used by the Han in trade and given as gifts. Rich people were sometimes buried in silk as a demonstration of their wealth. It also had practical uses, and the best musical instruments, fishing lines and bows featured silk threads. Poor people, though, still had to go fishing with ordinary string!

The Silk Road was so named because of silk's importance in trade between Asia and Europe. Europeans loved silk but couldn't figure out how to make it, so they were obliged to buy it from the Chinese. For hundreds of years the Han Empire used the huge revenues it earned from taxing trade along the Silk Road to wage wars, build huge cities and invent new things all thanks to the humble silkworm!
