Thanks to reforms put in place earlier by a minister named Shang Yang, the Qin state managed to use its resources much more efficiently than its rivals. It eventually became the most influential power of the last seven kingdoms in China.

Qin conducted many wars and won almost all of them! during the final Zhou period. By 236 BCE, one last great war led to the two remaining kingdoms being brought together into one in 221 BCE. King Zheng took the title of Qin Shi Huangdi which means the 'First Sovereign Emperor of the Qin'.

The Chinese Empire lasted for more than 2000 years until 1912, when the last emperor, Puyi, abdicated.

Abdicated

Abdicated means to give up a highly regarded position, such as a king or queen, or in this case an emperor.
