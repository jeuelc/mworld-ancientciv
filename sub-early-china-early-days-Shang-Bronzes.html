Shang Bronzes

By about 3500 years ago, during the Shang Dynasty, masses of bronze vessels had been produced.

The technology of metal-working was already developed in China in the Longshan period, some 4200 years ago. During the Shang period, people adapted the technology of pottery kilns so that they could generate the high temperatures required to melt bronze. The Shang Chinese made multiple ceramic moulds to cast the bronzes. Once the bronzes were finished and polished, sophisticated decorations concealed the joint lines between the mould pieces.

Bronze vessels were frequently given by kings or high feudatories (a feudatory is a type of land owner) to their subordinates, and these people often indicated their status with inscriptions. It made Shang aristocrats feel special to possess such artefacts, and they used them in special banquets to honour their ancestors.

Anyang, the ancient city of Yin, was the capital of the Shang kingdom. In the early 20th century, archaeological excavations uncovered thousands of inscriptions on bones and ritual bronze items, and also evidence of human sacrifices!
