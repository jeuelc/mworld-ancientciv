Have you heard of Kong Fuzi? Probably not, but you may have heard of Confucius that's how he's known in the West. 'Kong Fuzi', or Master Kong, is the most prominent intellectual figure of ancient China and his thoughts and practices remain important today in China and elsewhere in Asia.

Confucius was born in an impoverished noble family in 551 BCE. He served with the Duke of Lu but became disappointed with the political situation of his time, and left to travel among other leaders' courts in China. After he returned home he spent the last years of his life passing on old traditional teachings. He believed that the past was way better than his own time of political disunity.

Confucius's vision was based on moral principles and respect of the 'cosmic order'. There's a Chinese notion of li which explains it. Li means rituals or ceremonies, but it also involves respect for the five basic relationships that regulate humanity: between king and subjects, father and son, elder and younger brother, husband and wife, and between friends.

To Confucius a man is noble junzi not because of his birth but only when his actions are virtuous.
