Conspiracies, economic crises and rebellions finally caused the Han Empire to collapse in 220 CE. This didn't occur overnight. Rather, over many years the Empire grew weaker and weaker, and ambitious people decided that they wanted to seize power for themselves.

Many poor people got so fed up with paying high taxes and being mistreated by their landlords that they took up arms. For thirty years rebellions raged on and off across the country. Groups with funny names such as the Yellow Turbans and the Celestial Masters fought the central government. Finally, the Han Empire collapsed and power in China passed into the hands of local warlords.

The three biggest warlord states at that time were the Wei in the north, Wu in the south and Shu in the southwest, although other states arose along the fringes. The Xiongnu also managed to seize power in the north and founded their own dynasty, the Northern Han. It was a very violent and unsettled time for China!
