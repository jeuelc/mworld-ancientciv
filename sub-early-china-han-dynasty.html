Han Dynasty

The autocratic Qin government smoothed the way for the economic development and military conquests during the Han dynasty that followed. The Qin made it easier to integrate all the different cultural traditions from the mixture of earlier Chinese states.

Han emperors ruled China for about four centuries, with a short interval between the Western and Eastern Han, from 206 BCE to 8 CE and from 25 to 220 CE.

They maintained some of the Qin administrative structures but re-established the Confucian bureaucracy, returning some privileges to the old feudal families. A school to teach administrators was created on the Confucian basis of merit.

The Han dynasty was a period of expansion of the empire and of social unrest, but also a period of many innovations. Trade was opened up along what is now called the Silk Road, and there were major advances in science and technology, art and music, literature, and manufacturing of goods such as silk and lacquer.
