Civil War 1 - The Gracchi

 

The simmering tensions between rich and poor in Rome erupted into civil war in 133 BCE. Tiberius Gracchus, tribune of the assembly, proposed big agricultural reforms. At the following elections a riot erupted and a group of senators murdered Tiberius! This was the first civil bloodshed in Roman history (other than the myth of Romulus and Remus) and a huge trauma for the Romans.

But the family of 'the Gracchi' were not finished. Ten years later, Tiberius's brother Gaius Gracchus was elected tribune two years in a row. The Senate fearful of his popularity, declared Gaius an enemy of the state in 121 BCE. The consuls hunted him down, and, in their final conflict, Gaius killed himself. In the next century a number of ambitious generals who would once more urge the Romans to shed brotherly blood.
