There were many epic battles as warlords fought over what remained of the Han Empire. The most famous of them was the Battle of Red Cliffs in 209, between the warlords Liu Bei, Sun Quan and Cao Cao. The battle is still famous not so much because of the outcome of the battle itself, but because of the romanticised poems and novels that were written about it afterwards.

The Battle of Red Cliffs features in 'Romance of the Three Kingdoms', one of the four classical Chinese novels, written in the 14th century. This epic book tells of the deeds of the three warlords and their friends and helpers. Theres betrayal and love and lots of fighting just like in a good action movie!

Romance of the Three Kingdoms' represents the golden age of Chinese chivalry and is still read and enjoyed across Asia today. Chinese Children study it in school just as English-speaking children study the works of William Shakespeare. Most Chinese people can quote Cao's famous line, 'I'd rather betray others, than have others betray me'. Which would you rather?
